Discover Ondo State: A Journey Through History and Goverance

Overview

Welcome to the historical journey of Ondo State, a land rich in cultural heritage and significant milestones. Established on February 3, 1976, Ondo State emerged from the former Western State, unfolding a narrative that intertwines with the heart of southwestern Nigeria.

State History

Early History

Ondo State, often referred to as the "Sunshine State," was established on 3 February 1976, carved out from the former Western State. Its capital is Akure, the former capital of the ancient Akure Kingdom. The state boasts a mangrove-swamp forest near the Bights of Benin, contributing to its unique ecological features. It shares borders with Ekiti, Kogi, Edo, Delta, Ogun, Osun, and the Atlantic Ocean.

The state is the 19th most populated in Nigeria and the 25th largest by landmass. Dominated by the Yoruba people, the Yoruba language is widely spoken. Ondo State's economy is diverse, with a significant presence in the petroleum industry. Cocoa production, asphalt mining, and coastal activities contribute to its economic vibrancy. Notably, the Idanre inselberg hills, hosting the highest geographical point in the western half of Nigeria, add to the state's geographical allure.

Historical Transition

The state's historical landscape includes the initial existence of Ekiti State within Ondo State until its separation in 1996 by late General Sanni Abacha. Prior to the split, the state encompassed the entire area of the former Ondo province, created in 1915, with Akure as its provincial headquarters.

Ondo State officially commenced its journey on April 1, 1976, with Akure retaining its status as the capital. Like other Nigerian states, Ondo has ministerial offices representing the federal government.

Natural Wonders

Immerse yourself in the breathtaking natural wonders that grace Ondo State. The mangrove-swamp forests near the Bights of Benin tell stories of ecological diversity. The Idanre inselberg hills, reaching over 1,000 meters, offer not just stunning views but a testament to geological marvels that have shaped the state's topography.

Economic Tapestry

Beyond the oil-rich landscapes, Ondo State boasts a diverse economic portfolio. Cocoa production, with its historical roots, stands alongside asphalt mining and vibrant coastal activities, forming a robust economic foundation.

Diverse Cultures

The Yoruba-speaking populace, predominant in urban centers, nurtures a cultural legacy that seamlessly blends tradition and modernity. Festivals, art, and the daily lives of its citizens weave a rich tapestry, reflecting the vibrant soul of the state.

Historical Landmarks

From ancient structures in Akure to sites that commemorate pivotal moments in the state's history, each landmark is a chapter, telling tales of resilience, progress, and cultural continuity.

Cultural Heritage

Explore the customs, rituals, and artistic expressions that have been passed down through generations. Museums, traditional festivals, and cultural events provide a living testament to the enduring spirit of the people.
